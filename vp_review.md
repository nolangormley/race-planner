# VP of Engineering Review - Race Planner Web App

*Date: 2026-04-20*
*Reviewer: Nolan Gormley, VP of Engineering*

I just spent 80 hours this week grinding, and when I finally get a chance to look at the app you all have been working on for months, this is what I see? Frankly, I am appalled. 

This looks like it was generated in 20 minutes with a single LLM prompt. It is a derivative, cookie-cutter dashboard that lacks any professional polish or real-world utility. We are supposed to be building a premium, data-driven product, not a junior intern's weekend project. 

Here is my harsh feedback. Expect major overhauls immediately.

### 1. The "AI Insight" is Unacceptable Slop
The "Coach AI Insight" block is a massive, unformatted wall of text that literally just repeats the numbers already visible on the screen. It provides exactly zero high-level executive summary and is an absolute nightmare to read. This is the definition of "AI Slop." Our users are busy athletes paying for actionable insights, not a raw dump of tokens. Fix it.

### 2. Pathetic Visualizations
For a "data-driven" engineering product, having only **one single, non-interactive line chart** is an insult. Where is the visualization of the data? Where are the actionable metrics? 
We need:
- Interactive heart rate zone analysis breakdowns.
- Training load distribution comparisons over time.
- Performance trends (CTL, ATL, TSB) plotted meaningfully, not just thrown into text.
- More interactive graphs that allow the user to hover, zoom, and explore their data dynamically.

### 3. Zero Customization
There are no settings, no personal profile adjustments, and no way to intuitively modify the training goal. The only active button on the entire page is "Refresh Plan," which I can only assume triggers yet another lazy prompt. Users need to be able to customize their metrics, set specific race targets dynamically, and configure visual preferences (dark/light mode, dashboard layouts, etc.).

### 4. Static, Boring, Unbranded UI
The interface looks like you downloaded a free Tailwind dark-mode template and called it a day. There is zero brand identity here. The information architecture is just a long vertical list of cards. This is supposed to be a "Planner"! Where is the interactive calendar view? Where are the drag-and-drop features to adjust workouts? 

### 5. Information Overload vs. Insight Underload
You are drowning the user in raw numbers (CTL, ATL, TSB) without explaining *why* they matter or showing them in a meaningful visual context. Don't just throw data at the screen. Synthesize it and show the user what action they need to take today.

### The Bottom Line
This current iteration is unacceptable. I want a complete redesign proposal on my desk tomorrow morning focusing on high-quality charting, interactive customizability, and a UI that actually looks like we spent months on it. Let's get to work.
